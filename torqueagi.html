Simulation of Human Interactions with a 6-DOF Robot in Gazebo

Key Takeaways

  • Demonstrated proficiency in ROS2 and Gazebo for robotics simulation.
  • Showcased ability to integrate human models and define trajectories for realistic interactions.
  • Implemented advanced robot control algorithms for dynamic collision avoidance and task execution.

Objective

  • To create a simulation where a 6-DOF robot operates harmoniously with a human in a shared workspace, ensuring that the robot’s movements are safe and efficient, allowing the human to work without concern for the robot’s actions.

Approach

  • Simulation Environment:
    • Designed a realistic back-of-truck environment where a robot is tasked with loading and unloading boxes while a human assists with the process.
  • Human Integration:
    • Used ROS2 and Gazebo to integrate a human model into the simulation.
    • Defined the human’s movement trajectory using waypoints, ensuring dynamic and realistic interaction.
  • Robot Control and Perception:
    • Implemented a pick-and-place algorithm enabling the robot to efficiently load and unload boxes from the truck.
    • Utilized MoveIt2 and RViz to simulate cameras within the truck environment, generating a 3D point cloud representation of the workspace.
    • Developed a collision-avoidance algorithm that enabled the robot to dynamically avoid the human by steering clear of detected point clouds.

Results

  • The robot successfully performed pick-and-place tasks while the human moved within the workspace.
  • The collision-avoidance algorithm ensured the robot dynamically adjusted its path to avoid the human, even when the human deviated from the predefined trajectory.
