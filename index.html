Development of Longitudinal and Lateral Control Systems for Autonomous IndyCar Racing


What?

  • Developed a record-breaking autonomous racing solution for the ROAR Simulation Racing Series.

How?

  • Combined a Pure Pursuit controller for lateral control with a PID controller for longitudinal dynamics.
  • Emulated the racing vehicle in the CARLA Research Environment.
  • Used a Single-Track model to accurately predict vehicle dynamics and optimize the control strategies.
  • Implemented an offline global trajectory optimization for speed and path planning.

Results

  • Achieved the fastest time in the competition, setting a new record of 342.1 seconds for three loops on the Monza Circuit.
  • Outperformed all previous competitors by optimizing control and prediction models.
  • Demonstrated significant advancements in autonomous racing performance using model-free methods and optimization techniques.

Refining Autonomous Go-Karts: Advanced Control, Path Planning, and Localization Prototyping


What?

  • Develop and implement an enhanced braking system for a racing go-kart and improve localization precision for a fully autonomous go-kart.

How?

  • Go-Kart Braking System:
    • Identified the need to replace the existing braking system due to its slow response in competitive racing scenarios.
    • Designed a new system featuring a hydraulic pump for rapid brake fluid compression.
    • Integrated a PID algorithm to compare the desired brake pressure with the actual pressure measured by a sensor.
    • Employed Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) signals to adjust the hydraulic pump pressure based on the PID algorithm.
    • Stored and executed the control algorithm on the vehicle’s central processing unit for precise and faster brake control.
  • Autonomous Go-Kart Localization:
    • Worked on a fully autonomous go-kart project for one year, focusing on enhancing localization precision.
    • Integrated GPS and IMU data using a Sensor Fusion Algorithm to improve accuracy.
    • Chose the Extended Kalman Filter (EKF) for state estimation with multiple sensors.
    • Implemented the EKF in Python to fuse data from GPS and IMU, improving the go-kart’s positional awareness.

Results

  • Go-Kart Braking System:
    • Achieved a significant reduction in brake deployment time, from 1-2 seconds to 20-30 milliseconds.
    • Enhanced safety and control during competitive racing.
    • Demonstrated a hundredfold improvement in brake response time.
  • Autonomous Go-Kart Localization:
    • Successfully integrated GPS and IMU data, significantly improving localization precision.
    • The EKF-based Sensor Fusion Algorithm provided reliable and accurate state estimates.
    • Enhanced the go-kart’s autonomous navigation capabilities, ensuring precise and reliable operation.

Enhancing Aerodynamic Coefficient Prediction through Artificial Neural Networks


What?

  • Predict the aerodynamic coefficients of a wing (lift, drag, and moment) from its geometry using an Artificial Neural Network.

How?

  • Data Collection and Preparation:
    • Gathered data from an online database of 1600 airfoils, including geometry parameters and aerodynamic coefficients at different angles of attack and Reynolds numbers.
    • Used a scraping algorithm to download data files from the website, resulting in a dataset of 695,455 rows.
    • Prepared data by normalizing it to ensure faster convergence during training and splitting it into training (80%) and validation (20%) sets.
  • Model Training and Optimization:
    • Trained multiple neural network architectures to minimize mean absolute error (MAE).
    • Implemented a learning rate schedule with the Adam optimizer to adaptively reduce the learning rate, improving convergence.
    • Added dropout layers to prevent overfitting, though this increased training time.

Results

  • Developed a neural network capable of predicting lift, drag, and moment coefficients with high accuracy.
  • Achieved a mean absolute error of 0.129 with an adaptive learning rate schedule.
  • Demonstrated the effectiveness of neural networks in reducing computational effort and enhancing the efficiency of aerodynamic predictions in aircraft design.

Turbocharger Design: Boosting Performance with Precision Engineering


What?

  • Design, simulate, and test a car turbocharger fueled with hydrogen.

How?

  • Pre-scaled the turbine blades using Euler's equation and Cordier's diagram and determined blade thickness through polynomial extrapolations.
  • Designed the turbocharger turbine and volute using CATIA V5 Generative Shape Design and Part Design modules.
  • Tested the prototype through flow simulations inside the turbocharger (using StarCCM+).
  • Validated the design with a 3D-printed prototype.

Results

  • Developed a functional car turbocharger fueled with hydrogen.
  • Utilized a range of software and analysis tools to design, simulate, and test the performance of the turbocharger.
  • Achieved 68% efficiency with the printed prototype.
