Development of Longitudinal and Lateral Control Systems for Autonomous IndyCar Racing

Key Takeaways

  • Developed a record-breaking autonomous racing solution for the ROAR Simulation Racing Series.
  • Demonstrated proficiency in combining control strategies to achieve optimal performance.
  • Showcased ability to emulate and optimize racing dynamics in simulation environments.

Objective

  • To design and implement control systems that enable an autonomous vehicle to achieve competitive performance in a high-speed racing environment.

Approach

  • Control Strategy:
    • Integrated a Pure Pursuit controller for precise lateral control.
    • Implemented a PID controller for robust longitudinal dynamics.
  • Simulation Environment:
    • Emulated the racing vehicle in the CARLA Research Environment for realistic testing.
  • Optimization Techniques:
    • Used a Single-Track model for dynamic prediction and control optimization.
    • Developed offline global trajectory optimization for speed and path planning.

Results

  • Achieved the fastest time in the competition, setting a new record of 342.1 seconds for three loops on the Monza Circuit.
  • Demonstrated advanced control and prediction techniques that outperformed all previous competitors.
  • Highlighted the potential of model-free methods and optimization in autonomous racing.
