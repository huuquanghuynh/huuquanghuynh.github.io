Enhancing Aerodynamic Coefficient Prediction through Artificial Neural Networks

Key Takeaways

  • Successfully developed a neural network for accurate prediction of aerodynamic coefficients.
  • Showcased proficiency in data scraping, preprocessing, and deep learning techniques.
  • Demonstrated the potential for Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) models to enhance efficiency in aircraft design processes.

Objective

  • To predict the aerodynamic coefficients of a wing (lift, drag, and moment) based on its geometry using an Artificial Neural Network, reducing computational effort and enhancing design efficiency.

Approach

  • Data Collection:
    • Scraped data from an online database of 1600 airfoils, resulting in 695,455 rows of aerodynamic data.
    • Normalized data for better training efficiency and split into training (80%) and validation (20%) sets.
  • Model Training:
    • Experimented with various neural network architectures to minimize mean absolute error (MAE).
    • Used adaptive learning rate schedules and dropout layers to improve convergence and prevent overfitting.

Results

  • Achieved a mean absolute error of 0.129 for lift, drag, and moment coefficient predictions.
  • Validated the effectiveness of ANN models in aerodynamic analysis and their practical applicability in reducing design time.
